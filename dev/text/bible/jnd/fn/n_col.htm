THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Colossians
1:3b when
Or omit when. Ver. 9 proves this to be probably the true sense.
1:6c them
Or 'ye heard and knew.'
1:7d minister
Or 'servant,' diakonos.
1:9e filled
Pleroo, as in v. 25 ('complete'); ch. 2:10 ('complete'); and ch. 4:17 ('fulfil').
1:9f, 1:10f knowledge
Epignosis, i.e. 'full knowledge,' as chs. 2:2; 3:10; Matt. 11:27; Eph. 1:17.
1:11g strengthened
Lit. 'made powerful with all power.'
1:12h portion
See Note, Acts 26:18.
1:15i creation
See Note h, ver. 23.
1:16k by
En. Lit. 'in him,' in the power of whose person. He was the one whose intrinsic power characterized the creation. It exists as his creature.
1:16l things
Looked at as a whole: 'the all things,' as vers. 17, 20; Eph. 3:9.
1:16a things
See Note l ver. 16.
1:16b by
Dia, the instrumental power.
1:16c for
Eis, 'for.' These three prepositions, en, dia, eis, show Christ to be the characteristic power, the active instrument, and the end in creation.
1:19d Godhead
I add 'of the Godhead' from ch. 2:9, as it makes the 'it' in vers. 21, 22 seem less harsh.
1:20b by, 1:22b through
Dia, the instrumental power.
1:21e, 1:22e it
i.e. 'the fulness,' ver. 19.
1:22f irreproachable
'One against whom no charge can be brought,' as Tit. 1:6, 7.
1:23g the
Or 'in faith,' that is, full assurance of heart in the gospel.
1:23h creation
The word is used either of 'the creation,' or abstractly of 'the creature' as such; once for 'institution,' 1Pet. 2:13. Thus, in ver. 15, it is not of each individual as such, but of everything called 'creation' in its nature: see Heb. 9:11.
1:24i Now
He had previously become minister (diakonos, vers. 23, 25); he was now in prison, but rejoicing in suffering.
1:24k up
This refers to what lacked as yet; not to Paul taking 'his turn' as a person, in what is yet lacking.
1:25l dispensation
Or, 'economy,' as 'administration' in 1Cor. 9:17; Eph. 1:10; 3:2, 9; 'dispensation,' 1Tim. 1:4; and 'stewardship,' Luke 16:2, 3, 4.
1:25m complete
See Note e, ch. 1:9, also Note, Matt. 5:17.
2:7a up
'Rooted' is the perfect participle; it speaks of what had been done and continued. 'Built up' is the present, and is continuous: we might say 'being built up.'
2:7b assured
Or 'confirmed.'
2:8c *you*
'You' is emphatic; but the verb 'be' is in the future, implying real present danger more than possibility.
2:10d complete
Pleroo, as ch. 1:9. Or 'filled full,' referring to all fulness being in him. The fulness or completeness of the Godhead is in Christ, as towards us; and we, as towards God, are complete in him. 'Godhead' here is Theotes -- Godhead in the absolute sense -- not Theiotes, merely divine in character.
2:12e working
Energeia: active internal power, as Eph. 1:19; 3:7; 4:16; Phil. 3:21; Col. 1:29.
2:14f effaced
Used for annulling a decree of law; as 'blot out,' Acts 3:19; Rev. 3:5.
2:14g handwriting
Handwriting, obligation to which a man is subject by his signature.
2:15h it
Or 'in him.' In all the preceding passage the antecedent is God (ver. 12).
2:22i them
A term of Roman law: a class of objects as to the possession of which there were peculiar rules.
2:23k appearance
Logos: there was something to allege, to say for it.
2:23l flesh
The clause in parentheses is to show that the harsh usage of the body, and all the teaching of the seducer, was for (or 'as to') the satisfaction of the flesh; 'a certain honour' is what is due to the body.
3:5a death, 3;8a off, 3:12a on
The aorist: 'be in the state of having done it.'
3:5b desire
Or 'covetousness;' as Eph. 4:19 ('greedy unsatisfied lust'), and Eph. 5:5 ('unbridled lust').
3:7c which
Or 'among whom.'
3:10d new
Neos, i.e. young or fresh: see Luke 5:37, Heb. 12:24.
3:10e renewed
Renewed -- the present tense -- is from the same root as kainos, as in Rom. 12:2; 2Cor. 4:16; Tit. 3:5; and refers to what is entirely new in contrast to old.
3:11f everything
Lit. 'all things,' as 1Cor. 15:28.
3:13g forgiving, forgiven
As Eph. 4:32.
3:16h wisdom
It is a question of spiritual understanding whether 'in all wisdom' relates to 'dwell' or to 'teaching.' It may be the former: Paul taught in all wisdom (ch. 1:28), and in them the word was so to dwell.
3:16i psalms
'In psalms,' &c., may be connected with 'singing' rather than with what precedes: but in Eph. 5:19, 'speaking' is connected with 'psalms.'
3:23k heartily
Ek psuches: lit. 'from the soul.'
3:24a serve
Douleuo, serve as a bondman, see Note, 1Cor. 4:1.
4:5b opportunities
See Note, Eph. 5:16.
4:7c minister, 4:17c ministry
Diakonos, ver. 17 diakonia: see Note, 1Cor. 4:1.
4:11d who
'Who are such as have been.'
4:12e complete
Or, possibly, 'fully assured,' as ch. 2:2; 1Thess. 1:5; Heb. 6:11; 10:22.
4:12f will
Lit. 'in every will of God,' everything that is such.
4:17g fulfil
Pleroo, as ch. 1:9, and 'complete,' ch. 1:25; 2:10.
