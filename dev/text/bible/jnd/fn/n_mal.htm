THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Malachi
1:1e *
(The title of this Book, 'Malachi'), Said to mean, 'Messenger of Jah.'
1:7f offer
Lit. 'bring near,' as vers. 8, 11; chs. 2:12; 3:3, Lev. 2:8: see 1Sam. 13:9, 'hither.'
1:10a, 1:13a accept
Or 'be pleased with,' as ver. 8.
1:12b Lord
Some read 'Jehovah.'
1:13c torn
Or 'violently taken away.'
1:14d terrible
Or 'to be revered:' see Ps. 111:9.
2:7e seek
Or 'should seek.'
2:7f messenger
Malach, i.e. messenger or angel.
2:11g sanctuary
Elsewhere 'holy place' or 'holy thing.'
2:12h answereth
A circumlocution for 'all.'
2:13i further
Or 'secondly.'
2:15a them
Or 'did he not make them one thing?'
2:15b one
Or 'one thing.' The application of this passage to Abraham, which some insist on, is not to be admitted.
2:16c garment
Or 'who multiplies violence on his wife.'
3:1d messenger, Angel
Malach, i.e. messenger or angel.
3:1e covenant
See Ex. 32:34.
3:2f endure
Or 'be fit for.'
3:5g oppress
Or 'defraud:' see Note, Lev. 6:2.
3:5h right
Or 'wrest the judgment of the stranger.'
3:10i it
Or 'a blessing, to superabundance.'
3:17a treasure
Or 'my own possession,' as Ex. 19:5.
3:18b discern
Or 'shall again discern.'
4:6c earth
Or 'the land.'
4:6d curse
Or 'ban,' as Isa. 34:5; 43:28; or 'utter destruction,' as Zech. 14:11.
