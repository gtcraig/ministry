THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Deuteronomy
1:1e plain
The Arabah: so ver. 7; chs. 2:8; 3:17; 4:49; 11:30; Josh. 3:16; 18:18; Zech. 14:10.
1:4a and
See Josh. 12:4.
1:5b unfold
Or 'expound.'
1:7c lowland
The Shephelah, tract of fertile low country in the west of Palestine, as Josh. 9:1, &c.
1:7d south
See Gen. 12:9.
1:13e, 1:15e chiefs
'Head,' as Ex. 6:14.
1:15f officers
These were evidently civil officers constantly associated with 'judges,' as chs. 16:18; 20:9, Num. 11:16, Josh. 8:33, &c.
1:19g mountain
Or 'hill-country;' and so vers. 20, 24, &c.: see Num. 13:29.
1:26a word
Lit. 'mouth;' and so ver. 43.
1:32b thing
Or 'in spite of that.'
1:32c believe
See Note, Num. 14:11.
1:35d see
See Num. 14:23, and Note.
1:41e, 1:43e hill
Or 'mountain,' ver. 19.
1:44e hill
Or 'mountain,' ver. 19.
1:44a Hormah
'Destruction:' see Num. 14:45.
2:11b giants
Rephaim; and so ver. 20 and ch. 3:11, 13; Gen. 14:5.
2:13c, 2:14c torrent
Or 'valley;' elsewhere also translated 'river,' as in vers. 24, 36, 37: the word applies equally to the gorge in which the torrent runs; Gen. 26:17. See Note, ver. 36.
2:32a Jahaz
Or perhaps 'Jahzah' (Josh. 13:18, &c.); and so in Num. 21:23.
2:36b river, ravine
'River' and 'ravine' are here the same Hebrew word; and so ch. 3:16; Josh. 12:2, &c. See Note, ch. 2:13.
2:37c mountain
Or 'highlands.'
2:37d forbidden
Lit. 'bidden.'
3:9a Senir)
See ch. 4:48, Sion.
3:11b cubit
Or 'lower arm's length.' The cubit length is taken from the lower arm of a man -- as foot from our foot.
3:13c even
Or 'for;' cf. ver. 4.
3:14d Havoth-Jair
i.e. villages or nomadic encampments of Jair: some read 'called it after his own name, Bashan-Havoth-Jair.'
3:17e plain
The Arabah, see ch. 1:1.
3:21a go
Lit. 'pass.'
3:25b good, goodly
Same word as 'good,' Gen. 1:4.
4:3c Baal-Peor
Or 'the Baal of Peor.'
4:7d God
Or 'its god.'
4:23a forbidden
Lit. 'bidden,' as ch. 2:37
4:27b company
Lit. 'men of number,' or 'that can be numbered:' a usual idiom; see Gen. 34:30; 1Chron. 16:19; Ps. 105:12; and cf. ch. 33:6; Jer. 44:28.
4:37a countenance
i.e. in revealing Himself.
4:40b ever
Lit. 'all the days.'
4:43c plateau
Lit. 'in the country of the plateau.'
4:49d plain
The Arabah: see ch. 1:1; Josh. 3:16.
5:6a bondage
Lit. 'bondmen:' so chs. 6:12; 7:8; 8:14; and elsewhere frequently.
5:9b sons
Or 'children.'
5:10c mercy
Chesed, 'loving-kindness;' it is the same in ch 7:9, 12 (not ch. 7:2), Ex. 20:6, &c. See Note, 2Chron. 6:42.
5:11d idly
Or 'for an untruth;' so Ex. 20:7: see Lev. 19:12.
5:14e sojourner
Or 'stranger,' Lev. 20:2.
5:17f kill
Or 'murder.'
5:28a that
Lit. 'they have done all well that.'
5:29b continually
Lit. 'all the days:' so ch. 6:24.
5:31c, 6:1c commandments
Lit. 'commandment.'
6:9a gates
Gates of cities or of enclosures.
6:10b, 6:11b good
As ch. 3:25.
6:16c Massah
Temptation.
6:25d commandments
Lit. 'this commandment.'
7:5a Asherahs
See Ex. 34:13.
7:6b possession
Or 'a peculiar people,' see Ex. 19:5; Ps. 135:4; Mal. 3:17.
7:13c increase
According to some, 'ewes,' as propagating the flock: Heb. 'ashtaroth,' or 'astartes' of the flock; and so ch. 28:4, 18, 51.
8:9a copper
The word often rendered 'brass.'
8:12b fine
Or 'good,' as ch. 3:25.
9:10a assembly
Or 'congregation.' Same Hebrew word as congregation in Ex. 12:6.
9:12a themselves
Or 'are acting corruptly.'
9:12b way
Or 'quickly forsaken the way.'
9:15c in
Or 'on.'
9:23d word
Lit. 'mouth.'
9:26e Lord
Adonai, as ch. 3:24.
10:4a assembly
Elsewhere 'congregation:' see ch. 9:10.
10:22b made
Lit. 'put.'
11:1a continually
Lit. 'all the days.'
11:2b God
Or 'know ye this day (for I speak not with ... your God) his greatness,' &c.

Lit. 'was at their feet.' Ex. 11:8.
11:20a gates
See ch. 6:9.
11:24b Lebanon
Or 'to Lebanon.'
11:24c hinder
Or 'western,' i.e. the Mediterranean: so ch. 34:2.

Or 'beyond the way toward the going down of the sun which crosses the land of.'

The Arabah, see ch. 1:1: Josh. 3:16.

See Ex. 34:13.

Or 'because.'
12:13b offer
See Note, Lev. 14:20: so ver. 14.

12:23a life
Or 'soul:' see Lev. 17:11.
12:26b hallowed
Lit. 'holy.'
12:27c offer
'Do.'
12:28d hear
Or 'keep, and hear.'

Or 'thrust.'

Lit. 'consume by fire,' as often in Deut.

Lit. 'behold, it is:' see chs. 17:4; 19:18.

Imrah, 'a word,' 'speech,' see Note to Ps. 119:11. Occurs also in ch. 33:9.
32:4c work
As Isa. 45:9.

Lit. 'what is right,' 'just;' elsewhere often 'judgment.' Heb. mishpat.

Or 'sons.'

Or 'led him about.'

Lambs fed in rich pastures: so 1Sam. 15:9.

The upright people, ch. 33:5; Isa. 44:2.

Ps. 90:2.

Or 'shall have compassion upon.'

Lit. 'hand.'

Or 'at liberty.' The phrase seems to be a proverbial expression, in itself a paronomasia, to express forcibly 'the totality:' cf. 1Kings 14:10; 2Kings 14:26.
32:39d HE
'The Same,' 'the self-existent one.' This expression becomes virtually a name of God: see 2Sam. 7:28; 2Kings 19:15; 1Chron. 17:26; 2Chron. 20:6; Neh. 9:6, 7; Ps. 44:4; 102:27; Isa. 37:16; 41:4; 43:10, 13; 46:4; 48:12; 51:9, 10; 52:6; Jer. 5:12; 14:22; Heb. 1:12; 13:8.
