THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Isaiah
1:1d *
(The title of this book, 'Isaiah'), Meaning, 'Salvation of Jah:' see Ps. 68:4.
1:2a rebelled
Often 'transgressed,' as ch. 66:24: but has the force of 'rebellion.' It is transgression against authority to which one is rightfully subject. See 1Kings 12:19.
1:4b Ah
Or 'Woe to the.'
1:4c nation
Goi: the nations in contrast with God's people, used of Israel when disowned, as Ps. 43:1.
1:4d away
Lit. 'are estranged.'
1:5e more
Lit. 'add (or 'increase' revolt:' see ch. 31:6.
1:17f gladden
Others, 'set right,' or 'guide.'
1:17g to
Or, as ver. 23, 'judge.'
1:24a saith
A word introducing an oracular discourse, as Gen. 22:16. It is frequent in the Prophets.
1:24b hosts
See Jas. 5:4.
1:24c me
As 'comforts himself,' Gen. 27:42.
1:25d thoroughly
Lit. according to pureness;' the root of the word is 'purity.'
1:25e alloy
Or 'lead,' anything mixed with the silver ore.
1:27f with
Or 'delivered through.'
1:27g her
Or 'her penitents.'
2:2h *
For vers. 2-4 see Mic. 4:1-3.
2:2i days
See Heb. 1:2. A Hebrew expression implying the end of the period of law, when Messiah was to be introduced. It introduces the days of Messiah. It occurs Gen. 49:1; Num. 24:14; Deut. 4:30; 31:29; Jer. 23:20; 30:24; 48:47; 49:39; Ezek. 38:16; Dan. 2:28; 10:14; Hos. 3:5; Mic. 4:1.
2:3k peoples
See Ps. 7:8.
2:6l themselves
Lit. 'clasp hands.'
2:8m idols
Lit. 'nonentities,' 1Chron. 16:26.
2:9n man
Adam; an ordinary man, one of the people.

Ish: as chs. 5:15; 31:8. In these passages 'mean man' is Adam. Compare Ps. 49:2; 62:9.

As Note n ver. 9.

Or 'sights.'
