THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on 1 Samuel
1:1e Ephrathite
Or 'Ephraimite,' the Levites being considered as belonging to the tribe in which they lived: see Judg. 17:7.
1:2f Hannah
Meaning, 'grace.'
1:6a adversary
Or 'rival.'
1:16b Belial
See Deut. 13:13.
1:20c Samuel
Meaning, 'heard of God,' or 'asked for of God.'
2:1a said
Vers. 1-10 are poetical in Hebrew.
2:1b in
Or 'by.'
2:3c knowledge
The Hebrew is plural.
2:6d Sheol
See Gen. 37:35.
2:8e earth
Erets, 'land' or 'country.'
2:8f world
Tebel, 'habitable earth:' see Prov. 8:31.
2:9g saints
Chasid, 'pious,' 'holy:' see Note c, Deut. 33:8; 2Chron. 6:42; Ps. 4:3.
2:9h prevail
Or 'be powerful.'
2:13i servant
Lit. 'young man.'
2:15a burned, 2:16a burn
See Lev. 1:9
2:17b offering
Minchah, 'oblation:' see Note b, Lev. 1:3
2:22c served
Or 'crowded:' see Ex. 38:8.
2:25d judge
Here 'judge' as an arbitrator.
2:27e in
Or 'of,' 'belonging to.'
2:28f upon
Or 'go up to.'
2:30g saith
See Note, Gen. 22:16.
2:32h oppressor
Tzar, 'adversary.'
2:32i amidst
Or 'shalt see the distress of my habitation in.'
2:32a ever, 2:35a continually
Lit. 'all the days.'
2:36b into
Or 'attach me, I pray thee, to.'
3:1c frequent
Or 'was not published,' lit. 'spread:' see 2Chron. 31:5
3:10d stood
As Gen. 18:2.
3:13e vile
Or 'accursed.'
3:14f be
Lit. 'if ... be;' a strong negation: see Num. 14:23, 28, 30.
4:1a Eben-ezer
Meaning, 'stone of help:' see ch. 7:12.
4:2b spread
Or 'became general.'
4:4c between
Or 'on;' as Ps. 80:1, &c.
4:10d tent
Or 'home:' so chs. 13:2; 17:54.
4:13e the
Or 'his.'
4:21a Ichabod
Meaning, 'no glory;' or, 'where is the glory?'
5:4b fish-stump
Or 'Dagon.' Dag signifies 'fish.'
6:1a country
Lit. 'fields:' see Ruth 1:1.
6:4b them
Or 'you.'
6:18a Abel
Or 'peasantry: the great stone is witness:' 'stone' is in LXX, but in few MSS. 'Witness' is conjectural.
6:19b men
Some add 'fifty thousand.'
7:1c hill
Or 'at Gibeah.'
7:2d long
Lit. 'the days were many.'
7:12a Shen
Or 'the rock;' lit. 'tooth:' see ch. 14:4. ('sharp'), 5.
7:12b Eben-ezer
See ch. 4:1.
8:11c manner
Or 'right,' as ch. 10:25.
8:15a chamberlains
Or 'eunuchs.'
8:17b sheep
Or also 'goats.'
9:1c mighty
Gibbor: see Gen. 6:4
9:1d wealth
Or 'valour.'
9:2e Saul
Meaning 'asked for.'
9:5f, 9:7f, 9:8f servant
Lit. 'young man:' and vers. 22, 27.
9:7g if
Lit. 'behold.'
9:7h vessels
See Gen. 42:25.
9:10a servant
Lit. 'young man:' and vers. 22, 27.
9:15b apprised
See Ruth 4:4
9:20c all
Others read 'Whose shall be everything desirable in Israel? Shall it not be thine and all ...?'
9:21d tribe
Or 'clans;' lit. 'tribes:' see Judg. 20:12.
9:27a now
Or 'a moment.'
10:9b gave
Lit. 'turned.'
10:10c hill
Or 'Gibeah,' as ch. 7:1.
11:9a deliverance
Lit. 'there shall be salvation for you.'
12:3b ransom
Or 'payment,' 'bribe.'
12:7c still
Or 'present yourselves.'
12:7d with
Lit. 'judge,' shaphat.
12:11a Bedan
The LXX and other ancient versions have 'Barak.'
12:14b, 12:15b commandment
Lit. 'mouth.'
12:21c vain
Lit. 'emptiness:' see Isa. 40:17, &c.
12:24d for
Lit. 'with.'
13:1e ...
Critics in general suppose a hiatus, from the difficulty there is in translating the present text, which would mean, 'Saul was a year old when he became king.' The whole verse is wanting, in LXX.
13:2f Jonathan
Meaning, 'The gift of Jehovah.'
13:10a salute
Lit. 'bless.'
13:14b appointed
Lit. 'commanded.'
13:21c forks
Forks of three prongs.
14:5a crag
Strictly, 'tooth,' shen.
14:14b acre
Strictly, 'yoke,' i.e. as much as a yoke of oxen can plough in a day.
14:16c another
Or 'went on dispersing.'
14:18a God
* LXX has 'Bring hither the ephod, for he was wearing the ephod.'
14:21b Philistines
Or 'who belonged, as bondmen, to the Philistines.'
14:25c ground
Lit. 'on the face of the field.'
14:28d faint
Or '... this day! And the people were faint.'
14:34e sheep
Or 'goat;' and so ch. 15:3.
14:35a built
Lit. 'with it he began to build an altar.'
14:38b heads
Lit. 'corners,' 'corner-stones,' as Judg. 20:2.
14:48c valiantly
Or 'gathered a force,' 'formed an army.'
14:51d Abiel
Or 'And Kish was the father of Saul, and Ner ... was the son of Abiel.'
15:5a valley
Or 'torrent.' See Note, Deut. 2:14.
15:9b bearing
Or 'fatted beasts.'
15:9c lambs
Fat lambs fed on rich pasture.
15:12d Carmel
In Judah. See Josh. 15:55.
15:12e monument
Lit. 'hand.'
15:21a choicest
'First-fruits.'
15:21b devoted
Devoted by curse to destruction.
15:22c obedience
Or 'to hearken.'
15:23d idolatry
'Teraphim,' i.e. 'idols.'
15:24e commandment
Lit. 'mouth.'
15:32f gaily
Some translate 'in fetters;' as Job 38:31, 'the bands.'
15:33g above
Or 'among.'
15:35h and
Or 'because.'
16:3i name
Lit. 'say.'
16:4a him
Or 'trembled at his coming.'
16:7b countenance
Or 'aspect.'
16:7c appearance
Lit. 'the eyes:' see Ex. 10:5; Num. 11:7.
16:12d countenance
Lit. 'beautiful-eyed.'
16:13e David
Meaning,'Beloved.'
16:15f from
Lit. 'of:' so vers. 16, 23; chs. 18:10; 19:9.
17:1a armies
Or 'camps:' so ch. 28:1.
17:3b on
Rather, 'towards.'
17:4c champion
Lit. 'middle-man;' and so ver. 23.
17:10d defied
Or 'reproached;' and so vers. 25, 26, 36, 45.
17:17e quickly
Or 'run.'
17:26a, 17:36a armies
Strictly, 'ranks,' as vers. 10, 45.
17:29b me
Or, 'Is there not a cause?'
17:40a brook
Or 'valley,' as Deut. 2:14.
17:45b armies
'ranks,' as vers. 10, 26, 36.
17:45c whom
Or 'which.'
17:50d overcame
Or 'was stronger than.'
17:51e completely
An intensive form of the Hebrew verb.
18:5a forth
i.e., for warlike raids, as Judg. 11:3, &c.
18:5b, 18:14b, 18:15b prospered
Or 'behaved wisely:' see ver. 30; Jer. 23:5.
18:6c triangles
The word is differently understood; some interpret it 'noble songs.' It is as 'excellent things' in Prov. 22:20.
18:11d thought
Lit. 'said;' so ver. 17; ch. 20:3, 26.
18:11e wall
That is 'transfix him to the wall.'
18:14f and
Or 'for.'
18:21a time
Some connect 'a second time' with the preceding clause; others translate, 'by one of the two.'
18:30b forth
As Judg. 11:3, &c., see also ver. 5
18:30c better
Or 'prospered more.' See ver. 5 and Note, Prov. 1:3.
19:6d be
Heb. 'if he be:' see ch. 3:14.
19:10a wall
See Note e, ch. 18:11
19:13b image
Heb. 'teraphim.'
19:13c net
A protection from gnats.
19:19d by
Or 'in.'
20:19a day
Lit. 'thou shalt leave three days.'
20:19b Ezel
* Meaning, 'Departure,' or 'Separation.'
20:31a die
Lit. 'he is a child of death:' similarly in ch. 26:16. See Deut. 25:2.
20:35b place
Or 'at the time.'
21:11a sing
As Ex. 32:18.
22:2b spirit
Or 'discontented,' or 'sorrowful:' so ch. 30:6.
22:14a council
Or 'goes in at thy bidding.'
23:3a armies
As ch. 17:36.
23:22a subtilly
Or 'prudently.'
23:24b plain
The Arabah: see Josh. 3:16.
23:28c Sela-hammahlekoth
Meaning, 'rock of escapes,' or 'of divisions.'
24:7a checked
Or 'chided.'
24:11b for
Or 'huntest after.'
24:19c reward
Or 'Jehovah will reward.'
25:2d Carmel
In Judah: see Josh. 15:55.
25:3e Nabal
Meaning, 'fool,' 'ungodly,' 'impious:' see 2Sam. 3:33; Ps. 14:1; Prov. 30:22, &c.
25:3a churlish
Lit. 'hard.'
25:14b insulted
Lit. 'he flew upon.'
25:25a Nabal
See ver. 3.
25:25b folly
Or 'impiety.' The Hebrew word is from the same root as the name 'Nabal.'
25:26c avenging
Lit. 'saving' or 'delivering,' also in vers. 31, 33.
25:30d appoint
Lit. 'command.'
26:24a esteemed
Lit. 'great.'
27:10a south
Those portions of the Negeb inhabited respectively by Judah, the Jerahmeelites, and the Kenites: see Judg. 1:16.
28:1b armies
Or 'camps,' as in ch. 29:1.
28:2c person
Lit. 'head.'
28:3d necromancers
Or 'those who had a spirit of Python;' and so ver. 9. See Acts 16:16.
28:6e Urim
See Neh. 7:65.
28:13a god
Or 'gods.' Heb. elohim.
28:19b army
Or 'camp,' as ver. 1; ch. 29:1, &c.
29:6a me
Lit. 'good in mine eyes;' and so elsewhere.
30:1b south
The Negeb: so ver. 14. See Josh. 10:40.
30:14a south
See ch. 27:10.
30:26a present
Lit. 'a blessing:' see ch. 25:27.
30:27b Ramoth
Or 'Ramoth-Negeb:' see Josh. 19:8.
31:3c terrified
Or 'wounded.'
31:10d Ashtaroth
Or 'of the Ashtoreths (Astartes).' see Note, Judg. 2:13
