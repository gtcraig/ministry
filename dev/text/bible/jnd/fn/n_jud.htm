THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Judges
1:8a fought
Or 'had fought.'
1:9b south
See Gen. 12:9, Josh. 10:40.
1:9c lowland
The Shephelah: see Deut. 1:7
1:10d Kirjath-Arba
City of Arba.
1:11e Kirjath-sepher
City of the Book.
1:15f land
Or 'the south land.'
1:16g Arad
That part of the Negeb which formed the district of 'Arad:' see Josh. 10:40, and 1Sam. 27:10; 30:14.
1:17h Hormah
'Utter destruction,' as Num. 14:45.
1:19i valley
Or 'plain.'
1:24k guards
Or scouts,' as Gen. 2:15; 4:9.
1:27l villages
Lit. 'daughters,' as Josh. 17:11, &c.
2:3a sides
Others, including LXX, read 'they shall be to you for enemies.'
2:5b Bochim
Meaning, 'weepers.'
2:9c Timnath-Heres
Others read 'Timnath-serah,' as Josh. 19:50 and 24:30.
2:11a Baals, 2:13a Ashtoreths
Baal and Ashtoreth in the plural are constantly used as generic terms for the male and female divinities of the Canaanitish peoples, and especially of the Phoenicians. Ashtoreth, or Astarte, would seem to have been the moon: compare Jer. 7:18 and 8:2, with 2Kings 23:13, 14.
2:18b Jehovah
Or 'Jehovah had pity.'
3:3c ships
See Note, Josh. 13:3.
3:7a Asherahs
See Note a, ch. 2, vers. 11-13 and Ex. 34:13.
3:8b Mesopotamia
Aram-naharaim, 'Syria of the two rivers.'
3:15c left-handed
Lit. 'bound as to his right hand,' also ch. 20:16.
3:15d him
Lit. 'by his hand.'
3:16e cubit
Probably 'a short cubit.' The Hebrew word, found here only, implies no fixed measurement.
3:19f images
Or 'quarries:' so ver. 26.
3:24a summer
Lit. 'cool,' as ver. 20.
3:26b images
See ver. 19.
4:2c Harosheth-Goim
Or 'Harosheth of the nations:' see Josh. 12:23.
4:4d prophetess
Lit. 'a woman prophetess.'
4:11e Zaannaim
See Josh. 19:33.
5:1a *
Ch. 5 is poetical in Hebrew.
5:6b unused
Or 'ceased.'
5:7c villages
Or 'leaders:' so ver. 11; and Hab. 3:14.
5:9a, 5:14a governors
As 'lawgiver,' Gen. 49:10; Ps. 60:7, &c.
5:10b consider
Or 'sing.' As 'meditate,' Ps. 119:15.
5:11c acts
Lit. 'righteousnesses.'
5:12d captives
Lit. 'thy captivity.'
5:13e ones
Or 'against the mighty.'
5:14f root
Or 'seat.'
5:14g in
Or 'against.'
5:14h ruler
Or 'scribe.'
5:15i divisions
Or 'At the rivulets.'
5:16k of
Or 'the pipings to.'
5:16l deliberations
Or 'there was much debating.'
5:21m torrent
Or 'the torrent of combats.'
5:22n steeds
Lit. 'strong (or mighty) ones.'
5:30a each
Lit. 'the head of a man.'
5:30b spoiler
Or 'of the spoil.'
6:8c bondage
Lit. 'house of bondmen:' so habitually.
6:16a certainly
As Ex. 3:12.
6:18b present
Minchah: see Note f, Lev. 2:1.
6:24c Jehovah-shalom
'Jehovah of peace.'
6:25d Asherah
See Note, Ex. 34:13.
6:26e manner
As Lev. 24:6.
6:26f up
Olah, as Lev. 14:20.
6:32a Jerubbaal
Meaning, 'Let Baal plead, or contend.'
6:32b with
Or 'contend against.'
6:33c over
i.e. 'went over the Jordan.'
6:38d a
Lit. 'the.'
7:1e Harod
* Meaning, 'trembling,' as 'afraid,' ver. 3.
7:11a outside
Lit. 'extremity.'
7:19b watch
i.e. midnight: see Lam. 2:19 and 1Sam. 11:11.
7:22a border
Or 'declivity.'
7:25b, 8:3b Zeeb, 8:5b Midian
See Ps. 83:11.
8:6c hands
Lit. 'palms;' and so ver. 15.
8:13a Heres
Some read 'before sunrise.'
8:15b weary
Or 'faint.'
8:24c earrings
Rings for the ears or nose.
8:33a Baal-Berith
'Baal (Lord) of the covenant.'
9:2b citizens
Strictly, 'masters,' 'possessors,' and throughout this chapter, 14 times. See Josh. 24:11.
9:6c Millo
Or 'the citadel:' see 2Sam. 5:9 and 1Chron. 11:8.
9:6d memorial-oak
Or 'garrison.'
9:16e sincerely
Lit. 'in perfectness:' see Josh. 24:14.
9:19a sincerely
Lit. 'in perfectness:' see Josh. 24:14.
9:27b rejoicings
Properly 'songs of praise.'
9:29c to
Or 'of.'
9:31d up
Or 'stir up.'
9:40a wounded
Or 'slain.'
9:44b forward
Elsewhere 'spread themselves.'
9:46c, 9:47 men
See Note, ver. 2; 'men' here is the same in Hebrew as 'citizens' throughout the chapter.
9:46d Berith
Meaning, 'Covenant:' see ch. 8:33.
9:48e axe
Or 'axes.'
9:53f handmill
See Deut. 24:6.
10:4a Jair
Or 'Havoth-Jair:' see Deut. 3:14.
10:6b evil
Or 'did evil more and more:' see Hos. 13:2.
10:6c Baals, Ashtoreths
See Note, ch. 2:11-13.
11:2a woman
Perhaps 'a foreign woman.'
11:3b expeditions
Lit. 'went out;' a technical expression for predatory raids.
11:10c witness
Lit. 'hearer.'
11:18d east
Lit.'sun-rising.'
11:33a Abel-Cheramim
Or 'the plain of the vineyards.'
11:34b dances
Elsewhere 'dance choirs.'
12:2a strife
Lit. 'a man of strife.'
12:6b Shibboleth
Meaning, 'stream' or 'flood.'
13:1c evil
See ch. 10:6.
13:5a Nazarite
See Num. 6:2.
13:16b offer
As ch. 6:26.
13:18c askest
See Note, Gen. 32:29.
13:18d wonderful
See Isa. 9:6.
13:25a move
The Hebrew word is expressive of powerful emotion. See Gen. 41:8; Dan. 2:1, 3, where it is translated 'troubled.'
13:25b Mahaneh-Dan
Or 'in the camp of Dan.'
14:19a slew
Lit. 'smote.'
15:3b them
Or 'of them.'
15:3c toward
Or 'shall be more blameless than.'
15:16a heap
There is here a play on words, the Hebrew word for 'ass' and 'heap' being the same.
15:17b Ramath-Lehi
Meaning, 'hill of the jawbone.'
15:19c En-hakkore
Meaning, 'the caller's spring.'
16:1d harlot
Lit. 'a woman, a harlot.'
16:5e overpower
Or 'afflict;' and so vers. 6, 19.
16:6a, 16:19a overpower
Or 'afflict,' as ver. 5.
16:21b fetters
Or 'two chains:' the word is dual, and implies fetters or manacles in two parts, or in pairs.
16:28a eyes
Or 'that I may take vengeance for one of my eyes.'
17:3b had
Others read 'have.'
17:3c dedicated
Lit. 'sanctified.' Others read 'I have wholly sanctified.'
17:4d were
Lit. 'it was;' probably the 'molten image' served merely as pedestal for the 'graven image,' thus forming in reality but one complete image: see ch. 18:20, 31.
17:4e Micah
Found in the forms Mica, Micaiah, Micajahu, meaning, 'who is like Jah.' See Mic. 7:18.
17:5f consecrated
See Ex. 28:41: so ver. 12.
18:12a Mahaneh-Dan
Meaning, 'camp of Dan,' as ch. 13:25.
18:15b welfare
Lit. 'peace.'
18:18c these
i.e. the five men.
18:21d baggage
Or 'precious things.'
18:30a Moses
The LXX &c. read 'Manasseh,' but Jewish authority, followed by Jerome, supports the opinion that the text was corrupted.
19:2b months
Or 'a year and four months.'
19:3c her
Lit. 'speak to her heart.'
19:3d servant
Strictly, 'young man:' so vers. 9, 11, 13.
19:8e afternoon
Lit. 'till the day declined.'
19:15a place
Or 'broadway.'
19:18b with
Lit. 'walk with.'
19:22c Belial
i.e. of iniquity. It is literally 'men, sons of Belial,' according to a usual Hebrew idiom: see ver. 23.
20:1a Mizpah
Called 'Mizpeh' in Josh. 18:26.
20:2b heads
Lit. 'corners,' as 1Sam. 14:38; Isa. 28:16
20:5c citizens
lit. 'masters' or 'possessors,' as ch. 9:2.
20:9d it
Or 'we will deal with it according as the lot shall decide.'
20:10e Gibeah
Heb. Geba, as it is in ver. 33; but in the second place probably a distinct city.
20:12f families
Lit. 'tribes.'
20:13g Belial
i.e. of iniquity.
20:13h away
See. Note f, Deut. 13:5.
20:16i left-handed
See ch. 3:15.
20:18k Bethel
Others translate, 'to the house of God:' so ver. 26.
20:28a it
Or 'before Him.'
20:31b in
Or 'by.'
20:48a cattle
Some read, 'from the city entirely, even to the cattle.'
21:5b oath
Heb. 'there was the great oath.'
