THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Philippians
1:1c ministers
Or 'deacons' (diakonos), see Note. Rom. 16:1.
1:5d fellowship
'fellowship,' as 1Cor. 1:9.
1:5e gospel
Or 'glad tidings,' as elsewhere.
1:7f righteous
Or 'I feel it righteous,' 'it is right:' see Eph. 6:1.
1:7g grace
Or 'fellow-partakers (sun-koinonos, as Rev. 1:9) with me of grace.'
1:9h abound
It is a question how far the abounding applies to the love itself; or, supposing the love, that it should grow in these qualities. It is, I think, determined to be the latter by Eph. 1:8. It means 'grow in these,' yet love that would grow in them.
1:13i Christ
I apprehend 'manifested to be because of Christ,' not to be those of a malefactor.
1:13k others
Or 'in all other places.' The praetorium was the Imperial guard or its headquarters.
1:20a earnest
Or 'constant:' see Rom. 8:19: but 'earnest' gives this idea here: 'the constant looking out that it should be so.
1:22b while
Or 'the fruit of labour.'
1:25c in
Lit. 'of.' 'Progress and joy' go together, not 'progress, -- and joy in faith.'
1:26d me
Or 'as to me.'
1:27e in
That is, the faith of the gospel being in conflict, they identified themselves with it, and entered into the struggle, 'striving in favour of it.'
1:29f Christ
Or 'in behalf of Christ,' the sentence being broken in upon and resumed.
2:1g comfort
'Encouragement,' by word or any way. The apostle beautifully alludes to what he felt by their sending supply to him from so far; and adds, If you want me to be fully happy, be at one among yourselves.
2:2h that
'That' depends on an understood exhortation.
2:3i nothing
Or 'nothing' may be joined with 'thinking,' leaving out 'let,' and 'be.'
2:4k qualities
Or 'advantages.'
2:5l you
Or 'be found amongst you.'
2:6m rapine
Or 'esteem it rapine.'
2:7n himself
I have no doubt all this is in contrast with the first Adam. The history of the second man is that he made himself of no reputation (or emptied himself) in becoming a man.
2:7o place
Genomenos, aorist participle, the same word as is translated 'becoming' in v. 8. 'Being made' is wrong; it is becoming what he was not before.
2:9a highly
Or 'has highly exalted him, and given him.'
2:12b out
'To work out into result,' as Rom. 7:8, 13, 15.
2:13c works, working
Internal operation of power, though seen in results, as Matt. 14:2; Col. 1:29. Not as 'work out' in ver. 12.
2:15d generation
See Deut. 32:5, LXX.
2:15e appear
'Appear;' used in the middle voice for the rising or appearing of the heavenly bodies; so here with 'lights,' i.e. 'luminaries,' as Rev. 21:11.
2:16f vain
This is the other alternative to 'coming and finding them walking well,' as he had said in ch. 1:27.
2:25g minister
Leitourgos: as Rom. 15:16. A kind of official person in service.
2:27h death
'Almost the same as dead,' or 'in like manner;' as Heb. 2:14, where the object is not the essential sameness, but the similarity of the form and manner.
2:28i rejoice
Or 'seeing him again ye might rejoice.'
3:3a God
T.R. reads 'God in spirit.' The reading was in question as early as Ambrose and Augustine. Augustine reads both. Ambrose, till the Benedictine edition, was given as reading Theo(i), 'serving God the Spirit:' but they give Theou, 'serving the Spirit of God.' The diplomatic evidence is in favour of Theou, 'who worship by the Spirit of God:' but I do not feel assured of its correctness. {aleph} has Theou; but after all {aleph} is only an Alexandrian witness of the completest kind. But it is anything but a correct manuscript. In Revelation it is very incorrect indeed. D and P (in Tisch. M.S.I.) read Theo-(i), and so Am Syrr.
3:6b found
Genomenos, from ginomai, 'become,' see ch. 2:7, Note o.
3:8c Christ
Or 'have Christ for my gain:' see Note, Gal. 2:4.
3:9d be
Or 'that which is.'
3:9e through
Epi, conditioned by: see Note n, Rom. 10:19.
3:12f also
Epi, with dative, 'that I have been taken possession of' is the condition in virtue of which he expected the result. 'He would get possession ' but 'also he had been taken possession of:' see Note n, Rom. 10:19. Compare Rom. 5:12, 'for that all have sinned.'
3:15g perfect
Or 'full-grown,' as 1Cor. 14:20.
3:20h commonwealth
'Commonwealth' does not satisfy me, but 'citizenship' gives a different idea. 'Conversation' is wrong, though it be a practical consequence. It is 'associations of life,' as, 'I am born an Englishman.'
4:3a them
Or 'those women;' 'them' is feminine.
4:3b who
i e. who are such as, haitines.
4:5c gentleness
Or, 'moderation,' 'mildness,' not insisting on one's rights. As 'gentle,' Jas. 3:17.
4:6d to
'Before,' pros: see 'at,' Matt. 21:1. They were to address their requests to him.
4:8e noble
Or 'venerable,' as 'grave,' 1Tim. 3:8.
4:8f amiable
Or 'lovable.'
4:10g surely
I have translated this phrase 'though surely,' as giving the sense. It qualifies the apparent reproach of the apostle that they had revived their thinking of him: 'I say that ye have begun again, but I do not mean ye did not think of me, only ye had no good occasion to show it.'
4:15h came
Or 'had come.'
4:16i need
As 'wants,' Acts 20:34.
4:20a be
Lit. 'the glory' (with an emphatic article), the due divine glory; but 'the glory' in English rather means the glory of that of which he had been speaking.
