THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Esther
1:1b Ahasuerus
i.e. Xerxes, see Ezra 7:1.
1:2c fortress
Or 'fortified palace.'
1:3d feast
Specially for drinking, as elsewhere.
1:3e power
Or 'army.'
1:6a marble
Or 'mother of pearl.'
1:7b bounty
Or 'power;' lit. 'hand;' and so ch. 2:18.
1:10c chamberlains
Or 'eunuchs;' and so vers. 12, 15, &c.
1:22a people
i.e. 'should speak his own tongue.'
2:2b servants
Lit. 'young men.'
2:3c custody
Lit. 'under the hand.'
2:3d purification
Or 'preparation.'
2:6e Jeconiah
Jehoiachin, 2Kings 24:6, &c.
2:9f portions
Or 'food.'
2:10g birth
Or 'kindred:' see Num. 10:30; Ruth 2:11, 'nativity.'
2:12h manner
Lit.'law.'
2:18a bounty
Or 'power.'
3:1b Agagite
'Agag' is probably a title of the kings of Amalek.
3:4c matters
or 'words.'
3:7d month
Lit. 'from day to day, and from month to month.'
3:10a enemy
Or 'oppressor,' 'adversary.' Tzar, as ch. 7:4, 6; see Ex. 23:22, Ps. 8:2.
3:12b governors
Or 'pachas:' so chs. 8:9; 9:3.
5:14a gallows
Lit. 'tree,' as ch. 2:23: so elsewhere.
6:2b had
Or 'that they.'
6:3c servants
Elsewhere 'young men.'
8:10a, 8:14a horses of blood
Or 'mules.'
8:10b studs
Some explain it as 'mares.'
9:1c enemies
See Ps. 8:2, Note p.
9:3a king
Lit. 'the doers of the king's affairs:' see ch. 3:9.
9:24a oppressor
Tzar, as ver. 10; ch. 8:1: see Note o, Ps. 8:2.
