THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Zechariah
1:1d *
(The title of this Book, 'Zechariah'), Said to mean, 'Whom Jah remembers.'
1:1e Darius
As Hag. 1:1.
1:3f hosts
This name is in Zech. 52 times.
1:6a thought
Or 'devised,' 'purposed,' as Jer. 4:28.
1:8b night
Or 'that night.'
1:17c with
Or 'spread out because of.'
1:18d *
In the Hebrew, ch. 2 begins here.
1:20e craftsmen
Or 'smiths,' 'carpenters.'
2:4a walls
Or 'be inhabited as the open country.'
2:11b many
Or 'great,' as Ezek. 17:8.
2:13c up
Or 'is awaked,' as ch. 4:1; 13:7.
3:1d him
Or 'to be his adversary:' from the same root as 'Satan,' which has the article, as ver. 2: see Ps. 109:6.
3:5e turban
Or 'tiara;' or 'diadem,' as Isa. 62:3.
3:7f walk
Lit. 'give thee goings.'
3:8g portent
Men to be observed as signs, or types.
3:8h Branch
Strictly, 'Sprout:' see ch. 6:12; Jer. 23:5.
4:2a bowl
Others 'its bowl.'
4:2b pipes
Or 'seven and seven,' i.e. seven pipes to each lamp.
4:6c might
Or 'forces.'
4:12d beside
Or 'which, by means of ... tubes, empty.'
5:3e, 5:6e land
Or 'earth,' as 1Sam. 2:8.
5:6f resemblance
Or 'look;' 'iniquity,' LXX.
6:3a strong
Or 'vigorous;' others 'red;' so ver 7.
6:5b standing
Or 'presenting themselves,' as Job 1:6.
6:8c spirit
Or 'have satisfied mine anger.'
6:11d crowns
A tiara, composed of many crowns, as Isa. 62:3; Ezek. 16:12.
6:12e Branch
Strictly, 'Sprout,' as ch. 3:8; from the same root as 'grow up,' in this verse.
7:2a Bethel
i.e. those of Bethel.
7:7b south, lowland
Negeb (south): see Josh. 10:40. Shephelah (lowland): see Deut. 1:7.
7:9c Execute
Lit. 'Judge.'
8:6d wonderful
Or 'hard,' as Jer. 32:17, 27.
8:7e country
Lit., as often, 'from the land of the sun-rising and from the land of the sun-setting.'
8:8a God
See Note, Ezek. 11:20.,
8:10b distress
Or 'oppressor,' Tzar, as Ps. 8:2, 'adversary.'
8:16c execute
'Judge,' as ch. 7:9.
8:21d speedily
Or 'diligently.'
9:1e rest
Or 'and Damascus shall be its resting-place.'
9:1f Israel
Or 'when the eyes of men and of all the tribes of Israel are upon Jehovah.'
9:6a bastard
Or 'a foreign race.'
9:7b leader
Or 'governor;' and so ch. 12:5, 6.
9:9c salvation
The Hebrew verb has a reflexive force here -- 'having' or 'bringing salvation by himself:' see Heb. 1:3.
9:10d river
The Euphrates.
9:10e earth
Or 'land;' but cf. Ps. 2:8.
9:12f double
Isa. 61:7.
9:13g Greece
Javan, Gen. 10:2.
9:15h bowl
As ch. 14:20: and see 1Kings 7:40: it was for receiving the blood of sprinkling.
9:16i up
Or 'sparkling,' 'glittering' -- 'crown' is nazar, as Ex. 29:6: see Note, Num. 6:2.
10:1k them
i.e. those who ask.
10:2l distress
Or 'are afflicted,' ch. 11:7.
10:4a exactor
Or 'ruler.'
10:6b again
Or 'cause them to dwell.'
10:11c of
Or 'with.'
11:2d noble
See Jer. 25:34-36; 30:21, Note.
11:2e strong
Or 'inaccessible.'
11:5f guilty
Or 'and do not suffer punishment for it.'
11:6g men
Adam, mankind.
11:7h poor
Or 'afflicted.'
11:8i vexed
Or 'grieved:' see Judg. 10:16; Mark 3:5.
11:10a peoples
Ammim: see Deut. 33:3; Ps. 66:8; 67:3, 5.
11:11b knew
Or 'and the poor of the flock ... knew assuredly.'
11:16c away
Or 'is dispersed.'
11:17d worthless
Or 'idolatrous.'
12:3e wounded
Or 'shall be sore sounded.'
12:6f dwell
Or 'be inhabited.'
12:8g stumbleth
Or 'tottereth.'
12:11a valley
Or 'plain:' see Josh. 11:8.
12:13b Shimei
Or 'of the Shimites:' see Num. 3:18, 21.
13:5c, 13:7c man
Adam, ver. 5; Geber, ver. 7: see Job 3:3.
14:5a by
Or 'into.'
14:6b shining
Or 'the shining ones' (luminaries).
14:6c obscured
Or 'the light shall not be brightness and gloom.'
14:8d hinder
'Western:' see Joel 2:20.
14:10e Arabah
Or 'plain:' see Josh. 3:16.
14:10f dwell
'be inhabited,' as ch. 12:6.
14:11g destruction
Or 'curse,' Mal. 4:6.
14:12h their
Lit. 'his,' and so throughout this passage.
14:14i at
According to some 'against.'
14:18k be
Or 'this shall be;' or, according to others, '... and come not, shall there not be upon them ...?'
14:19a punishment
Chattah, 'sin,' as Prov. 10:16; 'Punishment' may be alluded to as what represents and marks the sin: see Lam. 3:39; 4:6; bearing the sin on himself in punishment, something like the sense of sin-offering. It must be so taken here; but so as to charge the measure and character of sin, not merely the fact of punishment. It is governmentally putting the sin upon him instead of taking it off: see Lam. 4:22.
14:20b bells
Or 'bridles.'
14:20c bowls
As ch. 9:15.
14:21d Canaanite
Or 'merchant,' see 'trafficker,' Zeph. 1:11; John 2:16.
