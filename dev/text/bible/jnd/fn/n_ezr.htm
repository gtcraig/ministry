THE HOLY BIBLE Darby's Translation
Darby's Notes on Ezra
1:1a *
(The title of this book, 'Ezra'), Meaning, 'Help.'
1:8a Sheshbazzar
This appears to be the Chaldean name of Zerubbabel, which may mean, 'Born in Babylon.'
1:9b chargers
Large shallow basons or dishes, either for the blood of sprinkling or fine flour offerings: see Num. 7:13; 1Kings 7:40. The word only occurs in this passage.
1:10c basons
See 1Chron. 28:17.
2:1d children
Lit. 'sons.'
2:1e province
i.e. of Judah: see ch. 5:8; Neh. 1:3.
2:2f Zerubbabel
Sheshbazzar; ch. 1:8; see 1Chron. 3:19.
2:20g Gibbar
Neh. 7:25: Gibeon.
2:43a Nethinim
Subordinate temple servants: see ch. 8:20; 1Chron. 9:2, and Num. 8:19.
2:50b Meunim
Or 'the Maonites:' see 2Chron. 20:1.
2:50c Nephusim
Or 'the Nephusites,' from Naphish: 1Chron. 1:31.
2:63a Tirshatha
Title of governor of Jerusalem under Persian kings.
2:63b Thummim
See Neh. 7:65.
2:69c darics
* A Persian gold coin weighing about 130 grains. See 1Chron. 29:7. The pound is the Hebrew maneh, about 50 shekels in money: see Neh. 7:71; Ezek. 45:12.
3:4d required
Lit. 'the thing of a day.'
3:8e superintend
Or 'set forward.'
3:9a Judah
Or rather, 'Hodavia:' see ch. 2:40.
3:11b alternately
See Note, Ps. 119:172.
4:6c Ahasuerus
Probably Cambyses, son of Cyrus.
4:7d Artaxerxes
Smerdis.
4:7e Aramaic
From here to ch. 6:18 the original is written in Aramaic.
4:10f cities
Lit. 'city.'
4:10a river
Strictly, 'beyond the river,' a technical name for the provinces on the west of the Euphrates. In English the expression has to be varied, in accordance with the position of the persons in question.
4:13b kings
Others, 'and thou wilt endamage the kings' revenue.'
4:24c God
Aramaic, Elah (Heb. Eloah: see Gen. 1:1). The parts of the book written in Aramaic are chs. 4:8 to 6:18, and ch. 7:12 to 26; they have always Elah. El does not occur in Ezra.
4:24d Darius
Darius Hystaspes, as ch. 4:5.
6:2a Achmetha
Ecbatana, capital of Great Media.
6:2b fortress
Or 'capital.'
6:3a decree
Elsewhere, 'gave orders.'
7:1a Artaxerxes
Most probably Artaxerxes Longimanus, son of Xerxes, Esther 1:1.
7:9b on
Others, 'he began the going up.'
7:10c ordinances
Lit. 'statute and ordinance.'
7:11d Israel
The Aramaic is resumed as far as ver. 26.
7:12e accomplished
Others translate, 'a scribe of ... of heavens: perfect peace.'
7:14f by
Lit. 'from before.'
7:17a offer
Lit. 'present,' as ch. 6:10, 17.
7:22b measures
Koreen, same as Hebrew cor: see Note, 2Chron. 27:5.
7:26c banishment
Lit. 'uprooting.'
8:18a namely
Or 'and.'
8:21b humble
Or 'afflict.'
8:21c right
Or 'plain.'
8:27a basons
See ch. 1:10, and 1Chron. 28:17.
8:27b shining
Lit. 'good shining.'
8:36c governors
Or 'pachas:' so Neh. 2:7, &c.
9:2d rulers
Segan, a Persian word.
9:8a nail
Or 'tent-pin:' see Isa. 22:23.
9:9b wall
Or 'enclosing fence:' see Ezek. 13:5.
9:13c trespass
Or rather 'guilt.'
9:13d less
Or 'spared us more.'
10:3e lord
The Hebrew text shows Adonai, 'the Lord.'
10:8a confiscated
Lit. 'devoted by curse.'
10:15b against
Others 'stood over.'
10:37a Jaasai
Or 'Jaasu.'
10:43b Jaddai
Or 'Iddo.'
